# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Prospective Wonderlings — people already attending or curious about Elsewhere (Nowhere), who want to know if Wonderever is their people. They're festival-experienced, open-minded, queer-friendly, community-oriented. They don't need hand-holding; they need to *feel* the vibe and decide yes or no. A secondary audience is curious Nobodies on the playa discovering the barrio for the first time via a QR code or link.

**Job to be done**: Understand what Wonderever is, feel whether they belong, and apply. One CTA: the application form. Secondary: contact email.

---

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Neon · Grungy · Wonderstruck

Wonderever is a 10+ year community of wonderful wanderers in the Monegros desert — they work hard, play hard, take care of each other in the heat. The tone is warm and self-aware ("totally not a cult"), with genuine human warmth underneath the irreverence. Think: a lovingly hand-painted neon sign in a dusty barrio that's been around long enough to feel like a legend.

**Voice**: Direct, slightly irreverent, warm. Uses "we" and "you" freely. Not corporate, not trying to be cool — just genuinely itself.

**Anti-references**: No clean startup landing pages. No card grids. No gradient-text hero banners. No soulless "community" stock photos. Nothing that could be any other festival barrio.

---

### Aesthetic Direction
**Theme**: Dark — deep desert night. Warm dark backgrounds (sandy near-blacks). The event is lived at night as much as the day; the neon glows hardest after sundown.

**Palette** (OKLCH):
- Background: `oklch(0.10 0.02 55)` — warm, dark, desert-sand-black
- Surface: `oklch(0.15 0.02 55)` — slightly lifted dark surface
- Brand accent: `#FF2358` → `oklch(0.55 0.22 15)` — hot pink-red, the neon heart
- Neon secondary: `oklch(0.82 0.16 135)` — dusty acid-green/yellow, like a battered glow stick
- Desert amber: `oklch(0.72 0.14 70)` — warm burnt orange for warmth and heat
- Text primary: `oklch(0.95 0.01 80)` — warm off-white, never pure white
- Text muted: `oklch(0.65 0.02 80)` — dusty mid-tone for supporting text

**Typography** (following the font selection procedure):
- Brand words: "neon / grungy / weird" → instinct says Syne or Space Grotesk → both banned
- Going further: The design should evoke hand-painted desert signage, neon tube lettering, early weird web energy
- **Logo / display**: `Monoton` (Google Fonts) — hollow letterforms that literally suggest neon tube signage. Perfect for WONDEREVER in a giant hero.
- **Headings**: `Bungee` (Google Fonts) — bold, architectural, sign-painter heritage. Bold without being generic.
- **Body**: `Jost` (Google Fonts) — clean, slightly geometric, warm; readable in photo-heavy contexts without competing with the display fonts.

**Effects** (all intentional, not decorative noise):
- `text-shadow` neon glow on key headings and the wordmark — layered warm glows in brand pink and amber
- CSS neon flicker animation on the wordmark (subtle, not seizure-inducing)
- Grain/noise texture overlay (SVG `feTurbulence` or CSS `filter`) across the page — the dusty desert air
- Full-bleed atmospheric photography with dark overlay
- Asymmetric section layouts — things break out of the grid intentionally
- Mix of enormous display type and small tight body text — extreme scale contrast
- Distressed/rough decorative lines, dividers, or ornamental elements

**Layout philosophy**:
- Single long scroll, cinematic
- No identical card grids
- Full-bleed sections alternating with tighter content pockets
- Type and image overlap where it adds drama
- ~65ch max-width on body text
- Left-aligned text predominantly — asymmetry feels designed

---

### Design Principles
1. **Feel before function** — The first 3 seconds must create an emotional response. Users should feel the heat, the dust, the neon. Only then do they read.
2. **One CTA, unmissable** — The apply button is the only conversion goal. Make it glow. Make it impossible to scroll past without noticing.
3. **Texture over polish** — Imperfection is intentional. Grain, grit, distress. The Burning Man world values handmade; the design should echo that.
4. **Scale as hierarchy** — Use extreme size contrast (giant display type vs. small body) instead of cards and boxes.
5. **Authentic weirdness** — The early-web reference is about personality and willingness to be unexpected — not about being retro. It should feel alive and handmade, not nostalgic.

---

### Technical Notes
- Stack: Astro 6 (static), React 19, Tailwind CSS v4 (CSS-first, no tailwind.config.js)
- `@fillout/react` is installed — use it to embed the application form
- Real photography will be provided; design should feature it prominently
- Deploy: Cloudflare Pages (static, no SSR)
- Image handling: Use Astro's `<Image>` component, register all images in `src/assets/images.ts`
- Performance matters — effects should use `transform`/`opacity` animations only, no layout props
